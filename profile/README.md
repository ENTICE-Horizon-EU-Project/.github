# ENTICE

## About ENTICE

**ENTICE – Enhanced Understanding of Trade Impacts on Climate, Industries, and the Environment** is a European research project focused on improving the understanding and modelling of interactions between trade, climate change, and industrial transformation.

The project brings together expertise in climate and energy modelling, economics, trade analysis, data science, and policy assessment to develop new evidence and modelling capabilities for the trade–climate–industry nexus.

## Exploring the Trade–Climate–Industry Nexus

In an increasingly interconnected global economy, the relationship between international trade, climate change, and industrial transformation is becoming ever more important. Trade policies can significantly influence environmental outcomes, while climate and environmental policies can reshape trade flows, global value chains, industrial competitiveness, and patterns of production.

Despite the importance of these interactions, the **trade–climate–industry (TCI) nexus** remains insufficiently understood. Important knowledge gaps persist due to data limitations, insufficient sectoral and regional detail, gaps in economic and integrated modelling frameworks, and the continuously evolving structure of global supply chains.

**ENTICE aims to bridge these knowledge gaps by advancing data, modelling capabilities, and evidence on the interactions between trade, climate, and industry.** The project investigates how trade policies affect environmental sustainability and how climate policies influence global trade dynamics. Through improved datasets, enhanced modelling frameworks, and fact-based policy analysis, ENTICE supports informed decision-making at the intersection of trade, climate, industry, and sustainability.

## Trade, Climate & Industry

ENTICE investigates how exports and imports, global supply chains, trade agreements, market competition, and different degrees of trade openness or fragmentation shape environmental and economic outcomes.

The project examines the complex interactions between trade and industrial transformation, with particular attention to the implications of climate policies for production patterns, sectoral competitiveness, and international trade flows.

## Climate & Environment

The project explores the interactions between trade and a broad range of climate and environmental dimensions, including climate change mitigation, biodiversity, natural resources, and energy resources.

ENTICE assesses how climate and environmental policies can influence trade patterns and how changes in international trade may, in turn, affect environmental pressures and the achievement of climate objectives.

## Challenges & Risks

ENTICE addresses key challenges emerging at the intersection of trade, climate, and industrial policy, including:

- Resource depletion
- Greenhouse gas emissions and pollution
- Biodiversity loss
- Carbon leakage
- Free-riding
- Trade fragmentation
- Uneven impacts of climate and trade policies

Understanding these risks is essential for identifying policy approaches that can support climate action while accounting for international economic interdependencies.

## Opportunities & Policy Solutions

Alongside these challenges, ENTICE explores the opportunities associated with international trade and cooperation. These include knowledge and technology diffusion, productivity gains, economies of scale, reduced pressure on local systems, and increased awareness of climate and environmental challenges.

The project also evaluates policy options related to climate change mitigation, environmental standards, sustainable resource management, industrial competition, and equity. By combining improved data with enhanced modelling and policy analysis, ENTICE seeks to provide robust evidence for the design of effective and sustainable policy responses.

## Open Data and Modelling

This GitHub organisation hosts code, modelling resources, data-related workflows, and other research outputs developed within the ENTICE project.

ENTICE is committed to open science and the FAIR principles, aiming to make project outputs **Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, and Reusable**. Where possible, research outputs are openly shared, appropriately documented, and made available in formats that facilitate their reuse by the research and modelling communities.
